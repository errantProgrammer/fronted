Maria Reiche Newman

The woman who unveiled the mysteries of the Nazca Lines

Here's a time line of Dr. Maria Reiche Newman's life

  • 1903 - Born on May 15 in Dresden, Germany.
  • 1920s - Studied mathematics, astronomy, geography, and foreign languages at the Dresden Technical University.
  • 1932 - Moved to Peru to work as a governess and later as a teacher.
  • 1940s - Began her research on the Nazca Lines with American archaeologist Paul Kosok.
  • 1946 - Published her first work on the Nazca Lines.
  • 1960s - Worked tirelessly to protect the Nazca Lines from threats and damage, gaining international recognition for her efforts.
  • 1977 - Published her seminal book, "The Mystery on the Desert," which popularized the Nazca Lines worldwide.
  • 1992 - Awarded the Order of Merit for Distinguished Service by the Peruvian government.
  • 1993 - Received the Grand Cross of the Order of Merit of the Federal Republic of Germany.
  • 1995 - UNESCO declared the Nazca Lines a World Heritage Site, partly due to her advocacy.
  • 1998 - Passed away on June 8 in Lima, Peru, leaving behind a legacy of dedication to the Nazca Lines.
